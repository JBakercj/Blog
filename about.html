About
I'm a life-long Utahn, regularly visited my dad in Lehi in 2001-2006, and became a first-time home owner in Lehi in 2021. I live with my wife, two kids (2 and 5), and my two dogs (Golden Retriever and Siberian Husky). For hobbies, I race my Camaro and Tesla in the state's SCCA autocross, I'm a bullet-biker, I enjoy Utah's public hiking trails, I'm a firearm enthusiast, I'm a cryptocurrency enthusiast, I like tinkering with smart devices, and I like politics.
Education
I graduated from Timpanagos High School in Orem and then started college at USU to study computer science. Near the end of my degree, I transferred from USU to attend UVU and graduated from UVU with a B.S. in criminal justice with an emphasis in law enforcement and a minor in computer science. After my Bachelor's degree, I went back to UVU and graduated with a M.S. in Cybersecurity.
Experience
I am currently one of the two At-Large members of the Utah Libertarian Party's Executive Committee, one of the three At-Large members of the Utah County Libertarian Party, and have served in the state executive committee role since April, 2021 and the county executive committee role since April, 2023. I have worked in the tech sector for 18 years, with 7 years experience in software quality assurance, 3 years of experience as a cybersecurity engineer in product security for smart IoT devices, and currently just over a year as a cybersecurity lead/architect handling all product security for an advanced robotics company where I design and set product security policies and standards. My B.S. in criminal justice, M.S. in cybersecurity, and my work in corporate cybersecurity policy gives me a unique perspective that I could bring to Lehi City Council.
